Program 01 · Flagship
GSSC
Worlds
The Global Proving Ground · Seoul, South Korea

The Global Student Startup Competition is the flagship annual culmination of the GSSF cycle. Undergraduate teams from regional qualifiers across six continents compete on the world stage at the Asian Leadership Conference (ALC) in Seoul — one of Asia's most influential global convenings. Every participant is audited against the GSSC Competition Standard to ensure the global stage maintains a world-class merit signal.

10+

May
Annual · Seoul

Team Structure

Teams of 2–5 undergraduate students. Delegations include 2–4 competing students per venture plus one funded student organizer who travels to Seoul.

Eligibility

Current undergraduates or recent graduates. Ventures must be pre-revenue, pre-incorporation, with work beginning no earlier than June 2025. English-language participation required.

2026 Schedule

  • Arrival: May 17 by 4PM KST
  • Pitch Bootcamp: May 18–19
  • Preliminary: May 19
  • Finals @ ALC: May 21
  • Departure: May 22+

GSSC x Region

In-person regional qualifying competitions select teams for GSSC Worlds. Early regions are seeded by GSSF or patron sponsors; maturing regions are expected to fund themselves by 2030.

Program 02 · Technical Engine
Global
AI Cup
Applied AI Competition · Regional → Seoul

The Global AI Cup is GSSF's regional activation and marketing engine. A $2,000 regional investment activates 10+ university campuses with 50+ participants each — moving students from AI consumers to AI builders through applied, in-person competition. The top 2 finishers from each University AI Cup advance to the Regional AI Cup to compete for cash prizes. By 2030, the top 5 regional champions advance to the Global AI Cup at GSSC Worlds in Seoul. The most capital-efficient activation model in the GSSF portfolio.

Program 03 · Theory to Execution
GSSF
Academy
3-Week Global Innovation Academy · Europe

A three-week, in-person Global Innovation Academy designed to bring together high-potential undergraduate students and experienced mentors from around the world to build early-stage, human-centered ventures. Taught by industry operators and mentors from Big Tech and top-tier VCs. The Academy is a feeder, accelerator, and ecosystem extension — not a substitute for GSSC Worlds. One team per cohort attends GSSC Worlds in Seoul with all expenses covered.

Participation Model

Invitation-led program with limited open application slots to ensure access diversity. Participants are selected through GSSF Friends & Family network invitations, university and mentor nominations, and a small number of open applications. Once selected, all participants are treated equally — no tiers, labels, or distinctions.

Pathway to GSSC Worlds

One Academy team is selected to attend GSSC Worlds in Seoul with international travel, lodging, full participation in the GSSC Pitch Bootcamp, and on-stage pitching at the Asian Leadership Conference — all expenses covered by GSSF.

Program 04 · Silicon Valley Bridge
GSSF
Ventures
Venture Access & Signal Platform

GSSF Ventures connects exceptional undergraduate founding teams to leading VC firms, accelerators, and startup ecosystems — with access fully funded. Talent is identified through GSSF's global network of mentors, who collectively see thousands of startups and founders each year. GSSF takes zero equity. The focus is identification, baselining, and signal creation — surfacing the world's best student founders and giving them a credible path to global capital.

What It Is

  • A venture access & signal platform
  • A global scouting & filtering mechanism
  • A bridge between regional ecosystems and global capital
  • A curated, relationship-driven model grounded in trust

What It Is Not

  • Not a traditional incubator
  • Not a long-term accelerator
  • Not an investment fund
  • Not a demo-day factory

The Golden Ticket

Ten Select Teams per cohort receive Golden Ticket access — an intensive in-person venture bootcamp followed by private pitch sessions with top Silicon Valley venture capital firms and institutional accelerators.

Global Scouting

GSSF Ventures leverages the Friends & Family network as its primary scouting engine. Trusted regional leaders recommend standout teams. Open applications are limited and intentional — enabling discovery beyond traditional startup pipelines.

Institutional IP

The GSSF
Global Standard

Four proprietary protocols govern all vetting, performance benchmarks, and operational growth across every GSSF program.

Proprietary IP · Values

GSSF Value Standard

The ethical and philosophical anchor. Mandates the Human-Centered mandate, the "Breaking Bread" philosophy of personal engagement, and the 85/15 Rule of financial integrity — ensuring impact-first operations at every scale.

Proprietary IP · Talent

GSSF Selection Protocol

A multi-dimensional vetting rubric to identify "Founder Market Fit," resilience, and execution capability. Built on discovery with leading global VCs and accelerators — effectively de-risking talent for Silicon Valley entry.

Proprietary IP · Competition

GSSC Competition Standard

The universal quality benchmark for all GSSF-affiliated programs. Whether a student is in a Regional AI Cup or GSSC Worlds in Seoul, the standard of execution and presentation is consistent.

Proprietary IP · Ecosystem

GSSF Scaling Method

The institutional blueprint for moving programmatic pillars from discovery to global density — activating undergraduates across the world through the Explore, Incubate, Scale lifecycle.

The 85/15 Rule

GSSF is committed to an Impact-First financial model:

  • ≥ 85% of all general funds directed to programmatic impact — student travel, lodging, curriculum, mentorship, and ecosystem development.
  • ≤ 15% reserved for legal, accounting, compliance, software, and governance.
  • Donor-earmarked funds are mission-aligned but exempt from the ratio denominator per board policy.
